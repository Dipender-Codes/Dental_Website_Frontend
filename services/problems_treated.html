Common Dental Problems We Treat

Our highly skilled team specializes in diagnosing and treating a comprehensive range of dental conditions using advanced technology and proven techniques for optimal oral health outcomes.

Tooth Decay & Cavities

Dental caries or tooth decay is damage to the tooth surface caused by acids produced when bacteria break down sugars in your mouth. Left untreated, cavities can lead to severe pain, infection, and tooth loss.

Common Signs

Toothache Sensitivity Visible Holes Pain When Eating

Periodontal Disease

Periodontal disease ranges from mild gingivitis to severe periodontitis, affecting the tissues supporting your teeth. This inflammatory condition can lead to gum recession, bone loss, and eventual tooth loss.

Common Signs

Bleeding Gums Swollen Gums Bad Breath Receding Gums

Endodontic Issues

When tooth pulp becomes infected or inflamed due to deep decay, cracks, or trauma, endodontic treatment (root canal therapy) is necessary to eliminate infection, relieve pain, and save the natural tooth.

Common Signs

Severe Pain Sensitivity Swelling Darkening Tooth

Bruxism & TMJ Disorders

Involuntary grinding or clenching of teeth (bruxism) often occurs during sleep and can cause tooth damage, jaw pain, and TMJ dysfunction. We offer custom solutions to protect your teeth and reduce symptoms.

Common Signs

Worn Teeth Jaw Pain Headaches Facial Pain

Tooth Loss & Replacement

Missing teeth affect more than just your smile; they impact your ability to eat, speak, and can lead to bone loss and facial structure changes. We offer comprehensive solutions to restore function and aesthetics.

Consequences

Bone Loss Shifting Teeth Bite Problems Premature Aging

Dental Trauma & Emergencies

Dental injuries from accidents or sports can include chipped, cracked, or knocked-out teeth. Immediate professional care is crucial to save damaged teeth and prevent complications.

Common Cases

Fractured Teeth Avulsed Teeth Jaw Injuries Soft Tissue Injuries

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about dental conditions and our treatment approaches.

How can I prevent tooth decay?

‚ñº

Prevent tooth decay by brushing twice daily with fluoride toothpaste, flossing daily, limiting sugary foods and drinks, getting regular dental checkups, considering dental sealants, and staying hydrated to maintain saliva production that helps protect your teeth.

Is gum disease reversible?

‚ñº

Early gum disease (gingivitis) is reversible with good oral hygiene and professional care. However, advanced periodontal disease can only be managed, not fully reversed, and requires ongoing treatment.

Is a root canal procedure painful?

‚ñº

Modern root canal procedures are generally no more uncomfortable than getting a filling. With effective anesthesia and techniques, patients are comfortable throughout, and the procedure relieves pain caused by infection.

What options do I have for replacing missing teeth?

‚ñº

We offer dental implants, bridges, and dentures. Implants offer the most natural results, while bridges and dentures are also effective depending on your oral health, lifestyle, and budget.

How can I manage teeth grinding (bruxism)?

‚ñº

Using a custom night guard, reducing stress, avoiding caffeine before bed, and improving sleep hygiene can help manage bruxism. In some cases, dental adjustments might be necessary.
